Fragrant Sticky Rice with Yuzu Chicken

Published on December 7, 2019
Yield2 servings
Estimated Time35 minutes minutes of preparation, plus 2-24 hours resting
Difficultyeasy
Special RequirementsPressure cooker, high-powered blender, fine mesh sieve

When I first came up with this recipe for fragrant sticky rice, it was such a revelation in our house that it’s become a practical staple on our table. Cooking it in a pressure cooker means that it doesn’t need to be presoaked and can be done on a weeknight. But rice alone does not a dish make. After experimenting with a few different vegetable and topping options, I finally settled on leafy chinese veggies and this diced yuzu marinated chicken, and the combination of everything is truly hard to beat. With the marinade coming together quickly the night before, this is a tasty and easy weeknight dish.

IngredientAmountProcedure
Yuzu Juice5 tbsp, about 5 fresh fruit
Mirin4 tbsp
Olive Oil1 tbsp
Lemongrass1 stick
Garlic4 cloves
Galangal or Ginger1 inch
Scallion Whites6-8
Boneless, Skinless Chicken Breast or Thighs1 lb
Kosher Salt2 tsp
Fresh Ground Black Pepper1 tsp
  1. Place yuzu juice, mirin, olive oil, salt, and pepper in a quart container or similar.
  2. Remove the outer layer from the lemongrass, exposing its core, remove both ends, divide into equal-sized pieces, crush, and add to the liquid.
  3. Remove the outer layer from the galangal, cut into rounds, and add to the liquid.
  4. Crush the garlic and scallion whites and add to the liquid.
  5. Place a cap on the liquid and shake vigorously to combine all ingredients and lightly emulsify.
  6. Cut the chicken into bite-sized cubes and add to the liquid, making sure all cubes are submerged.
  7. Cover the container and refrigerate at least 2 hours, up to 24 hours.
Sticky Rice1 cup
WaterAs needed
Galangal or Ginger½ inch
Lemongrass1 stick
Coconut Water⅔ cup, plus more as needed
Kafir Lime Leaves4 leaves
Sugar (optional)½ tbsp
  1. Add coconut water, sugar, and lime leaves to hopper of a high-powered blender.
  2. Remove the outer layer from the lemongrass, exposing its core, remove both ends, divide into equal-sized pieces, and add to the blender.
  3. Remove the outer layer from the galangal, cut in half, and add to the blender.
  4. Blend on high until no solids remain and and mixture is evenly combined.
  5. Filter liquid through fine mesh sieve, reserving the liquid and discarding the solids.
  6. Fill pressure cooker to its minimum fill line with water, place a stainless steel steaming rack or similar stand into the water ensuring its top sits above the water line, add rice and liquid into a flat-bottomed stainless steel bowl, ensuring the liquid covers the rice (if not, add more coconut water), and pressure cook on high (12psi) for 12 minutes. Let the pressure come down naturally when cooking is complete.

    Note: If you plan on doubling this recipe, the cook time for the rice will need to double as well.
Vegetable Oil4 tbsp
Chinese Leafy Greens, e.g. Snow Pea Leaves, Chinese Broccoli1 lb
Garlic6 cloves
Mirin1 tbsp
Cornstarch1 tbsp
Water2 tbsp plus 1 cup
Scallion Greens6-8 scallions
Toasted Sesame Oil1 tsp
  1. Preheat pan or wok over high heat
  2. Finely slice garlic into very thin slivers.
  3. Rinse leafy greens and trim away any thick stems.
  4. Cut the scallion greens on a bias into thin slices.
  5. Add 2 tbsp of vegetable oil to hot pan, add garlic and cook until fragrant and lightly golden brown, add the leafy greens, and cook down until greens are bright green and tender. They should shrink significantly in size. Remove from pan, set aside, and cover.
  6. Add remaining 2 tbsp of vegetable oil to pan and heat.
  7. Invert the chicken container into a bowl, remove the crushed aromatics, and add them to the hot oil. Cook until fragrant, then remove.
  8. Pour chicken and marinade into hot pan with now fragrant oil and cook until chicken no longer appears raw.
  9. Combine cornstarch and water together to form a slurry.
  10. Add ¾ of the scallions plus the water, mirin, and slurry to the pan, stir to combine evenly, and then cook down until most of the liquid has boiled off and the sauce has thickened.
  11. Add the toasted sesame oil, toss to combine, then remove from heat.
  12. Plate the chicken with the sauce over the sticky rice and garnish with remaining scallions.
